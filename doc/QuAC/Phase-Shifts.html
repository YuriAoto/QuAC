5.1 Phase Shifts

The first type of job implemented in QuAC is a phase shift calculation and this is for what QuAC was first designed (QuAC stands for Quantum Atomic Collision). In this jobs the radial Schr√∂dinger equation is integrated for positive energy values (relative to the dissociation limit) and the phase shift is obtained by the analysis of the behaviour of the wave function in the long non interacting range of the potential, u_l(r) = sin(d_l) j_l(kr) + cos(d_l) n_l(kr), where d_l is the phase shift, j_l and n_l are the spherical Bessel functions of first and second kind, and k^2 = 2mE/(h/2 pi)^2.

QuAC stores the phase shifts for posterior analysis.
